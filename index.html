Malaysia History

Malaysia is a Southeast Asian country inhabiting parts of the Malay Peninsula and the island of Borneo. It's known for its beaches, rainforests and mix of Malay, Chinese, Indian and European cultural influences.

Geographically

Malaysia is almost as diverse as its culture. 11 states and 2 federal territories (Kuala Lumpur and Putrajaya) form Peninsular Malaysia which is separated by the South China Sea from East Malaysia which includes the 2 states (Sabah and Sarawak on the island of Borneo) and a third federal territory, the island of Labuan

Bukit Tabur, Selangor

History

Bukit Tabur, also known as Bukit Hangus is one of the oldest quartz ridge in the world measuring 16 km long and 200 metres wide.Bukit Tabur is located in Selangor at the outer edge of Klang Valley approximately 20km from Kuala Lumpur. The quartz ridge is internationally recognized as a world-class geological site. The most important factor that makes it so significant and so unique is the sheer size of the ridge, and the fact that it consists of 100% pure quartz, making it the single largest pure quartz dyke in the world. Bukit Tabur becomes local favour hiking spot for regular and new visitor due to the easy access and natural richness near by the city.

KL Tower, Kuala Lumpur

History

Kuala Lumpur tower was constructed in 1991 and was completed in 1996. It was officially opened by former Prime Minister of Malaysia Tun Dr. Mahathir on 1 October 1996. This tower is the fourth highest tower in the world standing 97m above sea level at Bukit Nenas. This tower is also the second tallest tower in Asia today. It is used for telecommunication purposes and features an antenna that reaches a total height of 421 metres (1,381 ft).

Petronas Twin Tower

Kuala Lumpur

History

PETRONAS TWIN TOWER! I am sure everyone knows the tallest building in Malaysia and tallest twin building in the world. The question is how well do u know about Petronas Twin Tower? Let me lighten you with some amazing facts about it. Petronas Twin Tower is quite unique which has a suave architecture and a magnificent structure. The towers were designed by Argentine American architect Cesar Pelli. Petronas Twin Towers were known for being the tallest buildings in the world from 1998 to 2004 and it remains the tallest twin tower building in the world. Isn't that amazing? The twin tower consists of 88 stories and 32000 windows altogether. It is made of glass facade that was designed to resemble Islamic art motifs. The cost to build this enormous building took US $1.2 billion. Besides, the sky-bridge is at the 41st and 42nd level and it is 58.4m (192ft) long. The sky bridge itself weighs almost 750 tons. Petronas the national oil-company of Malaysia is the main occupant of these towers.

Mount Kinabalu, Sabah

History

Mount Kinabalu is the tallest mountain in Malaysia and the 20th tallest in the World. Mount Kinabalu towers 4095 meters (13,435 feet) above sea level. It is made up of molten rock formed millions of years ago. Mainly because of the biological diversity the mountain is listed as UNESCO World Heritage Site. It has also been designated as a Centre of Plant Diversity for Southeast Asia. Mount Kinabalu and its surrounding Park has a very wide range of habitats, from rich tropical lowland and hill rainforest to tropical mountain forest, sub-alpine forest and heath on the higher elevations. Because of the earth movement, The mountain is still rising at a geologically rapid rate of 5mm per year. In 1851, Sir Hugh Low, then the Colonial Secretary for the British crown colony of Labuan, credited as the first person to climb the mountain, took nine days to reach the summit plateau, traveling in a group of 42 people. Today more than 200,000 people visit the park each year.

National Monument, Kuala Lumpur

History

The National Monument is made up of 48,562 sq. meter space and 15,5 meters of height. It has 5 major components which are the monument, the pavilion, the surrounding gardens, the fountains and the war memorial. The National Monument was designed by an American architect, Felix de Weldon and was built at a cost of RM600,000. This monument was completed and officially opened on 8 February 1966, by Ismail Nasiruddin of Terengganu. Each of the 7 bronze human figures symbolizes leadership, suffering, unity, vigilance, strength, courage and sacrifice. The National Monument (Tugu Negara) is made in remembrance of the fallen ‘soldiers’ during the fight for freedom to independence in Malaysia. It represents the fallen soldiers during the 2nd World War, when Japan occupied this part of Southeastern Asia

National Mosque, Kuala Lumpur

History

The National Mosque of Malaysia is one of the largest mosques in Southeast Asia. This mosque is located at Jalan Perdana and was officially opened in 1965 with a capacity of 8000 worshippers per session. During the last few years, the capacity has been upgraded up to 15000 worshippers. The original structure was designed by a three-person team from the Public Works Department, the UK architect Howard Ashley, Malaysians Hisham Albakri and Baharuddin Kassim. The National Mosque was built on a land area of 13 acres with a Minaret of height up to 73 metres and 16 folds in umbrella dome roof. There’s a interesting fact to it. The 245ft high minaret which is in the form of an 18-pointed star, represents the 13 states of Malaysia and the five pillars of Islam. Visitors are welcome but are required to dress respectably and remain solemn

Niah National Park, Sarawak

History

One of the must visit caves in Sarawak is the Niah Caves. The Niah Caves is located at the Niah National Park. It is one of the world's largest limestones caves and the oldest caves in Malaysia with some amazing history that dates to the stone ages. The park’s main claim to fame is its role as one of the birthplaces of civilisation. The oldest modern human remains in Southeast Asia were found at Niah, thus the park becomes one of the most important archaeological sites in the world. Niah caves in not only known for its archaeology but also for its nature. It is a vast cave swarming with bats and swiftlets, birds-nests and guano, ancient cave paintings, a majestic rainforest criss-crossed with walking trails, abundant plant and animal life. The Niah National Park provides some interesting and impressive sights. The area was a major centre of human settlement as early as 40,000 years ago, and features one of the world’s largest cave entrances, Palaeolithic and Neolithic burial sites and iron-age cave paintings.gage, the Park HQ is a pleasant 45 minute stroll along the river bank.

Getting There

By Car

Niah is within easy reach of both Miri (109 km) with 2 hour and Bintulu (131 km) with 3 hour

By Bus & Taxi

Syarikat Bas Suria has a regular bus service to Batu Niah from the Miri Bus Station. The journey will take about 1 hr 40 mins whereas from Bintulu, it will take 2 hours to reach there.

From Batu Niah to the Park Headquarters, the Park HQ is about 3 km from Batu Niah. Taxis and tour buses will take you straight there, but if you arrive by bus or a share taxi, you have two choices. A motorised longboat from Batu Niah to the Park HQ or a taxi from batu Niah . The motorised longboats will brings you through delightful jungle scenery. A taxi from Batu Niah has not much to offer but it is a good idea if it is raining.

Take Bus No.33A from city centre to Pujut Corner Bus Terminal. From here, one can take any bus that goes to either Bintulu, Sibu or Kuching. Get off at the Niah Rest Stop. Bus fare is RM15.00 per person. From the here, take private taxi (about RM30 one-way) to reach Niah National Park HQ.
